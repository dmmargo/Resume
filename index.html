Personal Statement
I graduated from Cal Poly Pomona in May 2020 with a degree in Computer Science. Though I initially majored in International Business, I discovered my passion for engineering and design after attending a campus hackathon at the end of my first year. During the hackathon, I met three other students to work on an ambitious project: a virtual reality game. We initially had little knowledge of designing virtual reality games. But together, we learned new programming languages and concepts that helped us accomplish our goal. Not only did I present our game to judges, but I also shared it with students majoring in different fields to gain different perspectives on the project. Collaborating with my group and sharing my work with others made me realize that I enjoyed leading teams of individuals from various backgrounds.

My interest in leadership culminated in my senior year when I enrolled in a class called “Software Engineering.” Here, I was assigned the role of project manager for my team. By drawing upon my experience in front-end, back-end, business analytics, and qualitative analytics projects, I was able to partition my team’s workloads in the most efficient manner possible while also ensuring that they were involved in every step of the project.

In addition to my schoolwork, I was involved with numerous student clubs at Cal Poly Pomona. I engaged in many activities; a role of which I am particularly proud was my role as a Welcome Week Mentor, where for two summers I mentored and informed new students of the clubs, classes, and benefits the school offers while working with club leaders to coordinate plans. I was also the co-vice president and historian of the Computer Science Society, where I learned to manage resources, recruit club interns, and assist the club board with duties. Additionally, I was an intern and a secretary for the Bronco Esports club. I developed methods to efficiently host events of 500 attendees, worked with sponsors to provide students with giveaways, and worked with the campus organizations to book the appropriate rooms. I believe that school clubs are ideal spaces for networking and learning new skills, where students can express themselves in a welcoming and constructive communities.

Before long, I would discover opportunities for applying my leadership and management skills in the real world. During my third year at Cal Poly Pomona, I began an internship at Southern California Edison (SCE) as an IT Summer Intern. During that time, I created and presented a project for the Intern Project Fair and X-Change Program. My X-Change project premiered in the Continuous Improvement Recognition Event. Soon, I was promoted first to a year-round intern, and then to a full-time employee. For the past two years, I have collaborated on many projects and have managed access controls as well as the company’s subdivided SharePoint, which hosts around 400 users. I also help other teams as an individual contributor. To this day, I continue to learn new concepts and am eager to learn more about my capabilities.
